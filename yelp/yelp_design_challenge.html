The Challenge

How do we promot users to share recipes?

Analysis

Why do people share on social networks?

To better understand the problem, I started from asking questions about what and why do people share on social network to understand users’ motivation. I fount the sharing behavior lies in three patterns:

Interest

People like to share content that related to their and their friends interest or hobbies. For example, friends like to share cute pets video on each other’s timeline.

Record Life

People share photos, articles as a way to record their life. When they travel to a new city, they usually share photos as a habit.

Benefit

Users also like to share articles that their friends or family will benefit. For example, a group a designer will share design some useful tricks of design tools to each other.

UI Mockups

Laura opens the App and begins to explore the recipes. There is photos of dishes, the time needed to cook and review of each dishes, which makes it easy for her to decide which dishes to consider. She found the tags under the dishes are interesting and she noticed that there is “fitness” under one dish. This reminds her of her brother, who likes to work out a lot. “I should share this recipe to John, he might like it!” She opens that recipes and share it to her brother.

Laura finds one new dish she likes to try and opens the recipe detail. There are multiple pictures of the dishes and brief description at the top. She scrolls down to see more information and finds the “ADD TO BOOK” and “START COOKING” is fixed at the bottom of the app so that she doesn’t need to scroll down or scroll up the perform the action.

Laura starts to look at the ingredients and she finds that she can change the amount the ingredient according to how many people the dish going to serve. She feels like this will help her a lot because she won’t struggle with how much of each ingredient she should use because that she’s only cooking for herself.

At the bottom of the recipe, Laura found that there is a piece of the food knowledge about shrimp, that shrimp is very healthy and how it is cooked in the recipe can preserve the nutrition within it. She thinks this knowledge might also help others keep eating healthy. She clicked on the share button to share this knowledge with her friends. After looking through the recipe, Laura decides to cook this dish. She clicks on the fixed button at the bottom.

Laura enters the cooking session, the direction is separated into different steps so that she can focus on one step. She finds the photos of each step is really helpful. She can learn a lot cooking tricks from that. After she finished this step. She swipes the card to enter step 2.

Laura finds she can start a timer in the app when she needs to boil or cook something for the certain time. She noticed that there are 8 minutes left so she thinks it’s time to put another dish into the oven so that they will be ready at the same time.

Finally, Laura finishes this dish and she is pretty satisfied with her own cooking. She is about to take a picture, but she finds there is already an option of taking a picture of her dish. So she taps on the button and takes a picture of her cooking.

Laura’s picture is framed into a card with the dish name blow. And as usually she clicks the icon of Instagram to share the photo of her cooking as well as this recipe.

Strategy

How Recipe Share and Yelp work together?

Users will have recommended recipes that generated from the kinds of restaurant they usually go. On the other hand, Yelp can recommend restaurants/dishes according to the recipes uses cooked. With two application working together, they will both know the users’ preferences more accurately and users will also get more delightful experience.
