Project Overview

The Dead End Experience- the ‘Checkout Success’ Page

eBay is an online marketplace where millions of items are bought and sold every day by more than 157,000,000 active users. We were asked by the User Experience Design team at eBay to design a new mobile experience that 're-engaged' the user after a purchase. Our challenge was to produce multiple concepts that explored the space, highlighting a main concept.

Design Process Overview

Research Goes with Sketches

Every shopping experience at eBay is unique, however everyone who checks out experiences the same dead end - the ‘Checkout Success’ page. Though it’s great that shoppers have completed their shopping goal and made it through checkout, should their experience really be over? What kind of experiences should eBay build to re-engage our user base back into our ecosystem? How can we reinvent the Checkout Success page?

Reframe the Problem

Think about the Overall Shopping Experience

Instead of just focusing on the checkout successful page, we analyzed the whole online shopping experience to understand the user journey. We break it down to two loops and start to think about how to re-engage users.

Research

Design For The Revelers

After analyzing the four segememtation and some initial user research, we decide to focus on the revelers. The Reveler is guided by ideas and feelings—a personal process. They love to shop. For them, it’s about experiencing new things and ﬁnding new textures and smells. Then, the moment happens. Their ideas match a product they’re looking at, and they buy it on the spot. And far from regretting an impulse purchase, The Reveler always loves what they’ve bought too.

EXPLORE

Revelers often go to a new neighborhood and explore the little shops and boutiques. It’s fun, relaxing and they always learn something.

IDENTITY

Shopping is an extension of The Reveler’s personality. They seek for unique and surprising items and follow their own way.

ACHIEVEMENT

At the end of a successful shopping excursion reveler feel justified and happy. They’re excited to go home and create new combinations and possibilities with other items they own.
