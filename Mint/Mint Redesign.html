The Challenge

Create a pitch deck to revamp Mint.com

You work for Intuit and are pitching ideas on what to do to revitalize Mint.com. It’s languished for too long and needs a boost to compete with the growing number of personal finance apps.

User Research

Passive Role in Budgeting

Users want to use mint.com to budget and watch how they spend money, but they are notified only after going over budget. It allows users to take a passive role in their budgeting.

“I started to use it as a way to keep track of my spending. I wanted to see where my money was going exactly throughout the month.”

“I prefer Billguard because it helps me track bad charges and stay on top of spending at a super high level.”

“I didn't find myself changing behavior but simply being more aware of it.”

Our Solution

A Focus on Action

Increase User's Activities

By confirming transitions, users can monitor their money more effectively.

Plan Ahead

Allow users to add upcoming events to their budget so they can actively plan a budget for recreational events

Budget by Event

After user plan the event, mint will send users a reminder of the budget they have set for each event they have planned

UI Mockups

Jenna opens up her mint.com account to check her spending for the week. She finds that the Uber she used yesterday has been updated to her account. Jenna is able to confirm she made that payment.

While on her account, Jenna begins to schedule her Christmas decoration shopping. She plans to go shopping on Friday and plans to spend $55.

On that day, Jenna receives the notification reminding her that she has budgeted to spend $55 on decorations.

After a great and successful shopping trip, Jenna goes back to check her account. She finds her PartyWorld spending is posted to her account. She swipes and adds it to her budgeted Christmas event.

Jenna is excited because she finds that she still has $89 in her budget to spend on Christmas after buying her decorations today.

Use Story Telling to Present your Design
